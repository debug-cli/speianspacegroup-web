Founding Nation Seliet Union

The Speian Space Group traces its origins to one of the most consequential decisions ever made by the Speian Administrative Government — the creation of a dedicated scientific and engineering body to compete in an era defined by the race to space.

What began as a classified government accord became the institutional foundation for Speian aerospace ambition. Through decades of change — political, technological, and strategic — SSG has remained the enduring backbone of Speian space capability.

BASTION
Munar Surface Habitation Program
Permanent Settlement Ion Drive Resupply Surface Infrastructure ISRU Capable

BASTION is SSG's program for the permanent occupation of the Munar surface. Where IGNITION establishes the transit route, BASTION establishes the destination. The program encompasses the design, delivery, and construction of a modular surface habitat capable of supporting a sustained Speian crew presence on the Mun indefinitely.

BASTION's logistical architecture relies on ion-drive cargo vessels for continuous low-cost resupply of consumables, equipment, and personnel between Kerbin and the surface installation. Ion propulsion's high efficiency over long transit times makes it ideal for the sustained supply chain a permanent habitat demands. In-situ resource utilization systems allow BASTION to supplement consumables from Munar regolith, reducing dependency on surface-launched resupply.

BASTION represents Speia's commitment that the Mun will not merely be visited. It will be occupied.

PHOENIX
Reusable Launch Vehicle Program
Full Reusability Two-Stage Architecture Propulsive Landing Rapid Turnaround

PHOENIX is SSG's next-generation reusable launch vehicle program, designed around a two-stage architecture in which both the booster and upper stage return to the launch site under propulsive power for rapid turnaround and reuse. The program directly addresses the cost barrier that has historically constrained Speian launch cadence — by eliminating the expendable hardware cost on every mission.

The PHOENIX booster performs a powered return following stage separation, executing a retrograde burn and precision propulsive landing at or near the launch facility. The upper stage similarly returns following payload deployment. Target turnaround from landing to next launch is measured in days, not months — enabling the high launch frequency that IGNITION, BASTION, and the broader Speian space expansion program demand.

PHOENIX is how Speia sustains its presence in space at the scale the mission requires.

Defense & Military Heritage

Forged in the
Seliet Era

"SSG does not separate the advancement of Speia from the security of Speia. These have always been the same mission."

Long before SSG was a contractor, it was an instrument of state survival. The Seliet Special Project Accord was not merely a scientific body — it was a strategic asset of the Seliet Union, operating at the edge of what was technically possible and politically permissible.

During the height of the cold-war rivalry with Slimeslyvania, SSG's predecessor organization conducted a series of operations that remain among the most sensitive in Speian institutional history. The Cripybin Covert Deployment — in which SSG engineering teams covertly facilitated the positioning of intermediate-range missile infrastructure within the Cripybin territory — demonstrated SSG's willingness to operate far beyond the conventional boundaries of an aerospace contractor. The operation was executed without public acknowledgment and remained classified for decades.

In parallel, SSG served as the primary technical development arm for the Seliet Union's strategic nuclear and conventional missile programs. SSG engineers contributed to the design, testing, and refinement of first-class ballistic and cruise missile systems — work that established Speian deterrence capability during the most dangerous decades of the inter-bloc conflict.

When the Seliet Union collapsed, SSG did not dissolve with it. The Speian Administrative Government retained SSG as its primary long-term aerospace and defense contractor — recognizing that the institutional knowledge, the cleared personnel, and the program continuity SSG represented were irreplaceable national assets.

SSG's commitment to Speia is unconditional. We were built in service of this nation's survival and will remain in service of its success — in whatever form that requires.

Government Contracting
Terms & Requirements

The Speian Space Group accepts government contracting engagements exclusively from verified state, military, and inter-governmental bodies. All prospective clients must meet the following requirements before a formal engagement can be initiated.

01 — Institutional Verification

Contracting body must provide official state credentials, ministry authorization letters, and a designated point of contact with verified governmental authority. Non-state actors are not eligible.

02 — Scope Declaration

Client must submit a written scope declaration describing program objectives, classification requirements, timeline constraints, and any existing infrastructure or assets relevant to the engagement.

03 — Budget Commitment

A confirmed budget envelope must be established prior to program scoping. SSG does not engage in speculative or unfunded program development. Minimum engagement thresholds apply depending on program classification tier.

04 — Non-Disclosure Agreement

All government contracting engagements are conducted under SSG's standard bilateral non-disclosure framework. Execution of the NDA is a precondition to any technical or program briefing.

The Mission

SSG's government contracting division exists to extend Speian aerospace capability to allied states and partner governments — not as a commercial service, but as a strategic relationship. Every contract SSG accepts must serve the advancement of capable, trustworthy spacefaring nations. We do not contract with parties whose objectives are incompatible with Speian national interests or the long-term vision of human expansion.

The Plan

Upon qualification, SSG assigns a dedicated program officer to the engagement. A scoping phase is conducted to define deliverables, milestones, classification tiers, and resource allocation. SSG provides a formal Program Execution Plan (PEP) which governs all subsequent activity. The PEP is reviewed and agreed upon by both parties before any technical work commences.

The Budget

SSG operates on a fixed-price contract model for clearly defined deliverables, and a cost-plus model for exploratory or classified R&D programs where scope may evolve. Budget structures are agreed in the PEP and reviewed at defined program milestones. SSG does not permit unilateral budget reductions mid-program — clients are expected to honor committed funding envelopes.

The Job

SSG delivers. From launch vehicle design to orbital infrastructure, from classified propulsion research to crewed mission architecture — SSG brings the full depth of its institutional knowledge, cleared personnel, and physical infrastructure to every engagement. Our record of program delivery speaks for itself. To initiate a government contracting inquiry, contact SSG leadership via Discord or email through the Contact section.

Launch Manifest & Pre-Launch Standards

A launch is not a single moment. It is the outcome of hundreds of decisions made correctly, in sequence, over months. SSG's launch readiness standard defines what a perfect launch requires — and what must be verified before any vehicle leaves the ground.

What Makes a Perfect Launch
I
Payload Integrity

The payload must be fully qualified, structurally verified, and encapsulated within its fairing to specification. No launch proceeds with an unverified payload. Mass, center of gravity, and interface connections are confirmed against design documentation before fairing close-out.

II
Vehicle Readiness Review

All propulsion, avionics, structural, thermal, and electrical subsystems must pass the Vehicle Readiness Review board. Any open anomaly must be closed or accepted by the chief engineer before the review is signed. No open safety-critical items are permitted at VRR.

III
Range Safety & Weather

Launch range must be clear of all unauthorized aircraft and maritime traffic within the exclusion zone. Weather must meet defined launch commit criteria — wind shear, upper-level winds, lightning probability, and visibility are all assessed against mission-specific thresholds. SSG does not launch into marginal weather.

IV
Propellant Load & Systems Activation

Propellant loading is conducted under strict hazardous operations protocols with dedicated pad safety teams. All systems are activated sequentially in accordance with the terminal countdown checklist. Pressures, temperatures, flow rates, and electrical states are verified at each hold point.

V
Flight Termination System & Final Go/No-Go

The flight termination system must be armed, tested, and confirmed operational. The final Go/No-Go poll is conducted across all SSG launch directors — Propulsion, Avionics, Range, Weather, Payload, and Mission. All directors must give Go. A single No-Go holds the count. There are no exceptions.

Requesting a Launch Slot

The Thunderbird launch manifest is open to government and commercial smallsat customers. To request a launch slot, clients must provide payload specifications, target orbit parameters, desired launch window, and budget authorization. SSG assigns manifest positions on a first-qualified, first-allocated basis.
